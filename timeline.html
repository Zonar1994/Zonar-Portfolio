"It's a Match" AI tools for Investigative Journalism

week 1/2
The project started off with a briefing with Marius Brugman and Berry Sanders, a Journalist and overseer of the “It’s A Match project. The overall objective was to make AI tools user-friendly for investigative journalists. Marius Brugman and Berry Sanders, our clients, wanted us to create an interface that incorporates AI capabilities. We collaborated with software and journalism students to achieve this. We had the option to either build a new tool or improve an existing one to meet the journalists' needs.
During the introductionwe were given the option to choose the direction we wanted to go as a group with a concept, several tools were presented as options for us to work on if we were interested. One of the ideas being considered is to develop an easy-to-use timeline. Our group found this idea fascinating and decided to focus on it for the project. We collaborated to brainstorm and explore the possibilities for creating a timeline.

Project Plan and picking a Data-set

As a group we created a project plan in order to scope out the entire project. This included our objectives, scope, deliverables and the a rough timeline of the project. With this complex project, I decided to formulate sub-questions within the project plan and added main questions as well. Triangulating the suq-questions seemed to be the most effective way on figuring out how to tackle this project.
At this point we also decided which data-set to pick for the timeline project. The corona curfew protest has been documented by many civilians through social media and investigative journalist have been trying to log each and every social media post/article. This dataset would then be used to find out how the protests spread that day. Therefore arranging this dataset in a chronological order would be one way of tackling this investigation.




What AI Tools can be incorporated into the workflow right now?

I explore the integration of AI into the workflow of investigative journalism, providing examples and references to current research and tools.
Research findings:
AI has transformative potential in investigative journalism, enabling new data tools for collection, analysis, fact-checking, and source verification. Nonetheless, it's crucial to recognize AI's limitations, biases, and ethical considerations. Despite challenges, AI has already made significant contributions and promises further advancements. As journalism evolves, AI will undoubtedly shape its future.

Object Detection

The "timeline" project is a concept that can put large sums of data into a time line. This allows an investigative journalist to arrange and view a large data set in a chronological order. In order to analyze these large sets of data and the put them in a chronological order, each data item should be analyzed by AI tools. These AI tools can range from object detection to complete 3D scan analyzations.
Research findings:
Overall, both Lerf and video-based crime prediction(deep learning) technologies have potential applications in investigative journalism. Leveraging the capabilities of artificial intelligence, will allow the timeline tool to arrange the data-sets in a chronological order.


The insights gained from interviews conducted with industry professionals

Including Harry from Omroep Flevoland and Judith and Pim from DPG Media.
The interviews revealed Harry's enthusiasm for AI in journalism, emphasizing the potential of an intuitive interface or timeline to enhance investigative journalists' workflow. The guide emphasizes the importance of a user-friendly and accessible system to ensure widespread adoption, particularly among older and conservative journalists. Furthermore, it emphasizes the significance of integrating AI into the timeline while maintaining simplicity to encourage journalists to embrace its utilization.


Designing the Timeline interface:

After gaining insights on developing a tool to enhance the workflow of investigative journalists, we moved on to the design phase. Using Figma, we created the initial design with a focus on data analysis, specifically the "Data Item" page. This page showcases the results of data analysis, verifying and organizing data items chronologically. The goal is to utilize AI to analyze the dataset, extract key details like time and location, and present them in a user-friendly format.
