Start-up Project

week 1/2
For my first project in semester 6 of Media Design, I get to create a startup company with my group! I'm thrilled to start this exciting challenge that demands creativity, collaboration, and critical thinking. We will also receive feedback from Hike One, a UX Design company, through weekly meetings. With Hike-One's critical feedback and feedforward, we can refine our ideas and make progress towards creating a successful startup.
As a group we decided to use the Double Diamond for the design process, all of my group members are familiar with this design process and therefore it was an easy decision to go for that design process.

Brainstorming new ideas

Our journey to create a start-up began with the generation of ideas and concepts. To facilitate this process, we experimented with various brainstorming techniques.

Ai Recipe Generator:
An application that can generate personalized recipes based of off user input.


At one point the majority of the group, including myself was really excited about the AI Recipe Generator, partialy due to the fact that the idea was generated by an AI, chatGPT and I have been really inspired by all of these new advancements in artificial intelligence.



Concept Statement:

"A application that that utilizes artificial intelligence to generate personalized recipes for users, providing an easy way to prepare meals that match their individual taste and preferences. With Recipeazy, users can find recipes that perfectly fit their personal needs and preferences."

Defining the target group audience

To get a multiple target audience research (library research, surveys, and in-depth interviews). These studies revealed the following:

  • Millennials (aged 25 to 45) are the primary target audience for our platform.
  • This group often experiences difficulties in finding suitable recipes and may have little time or inclination to put in a lot of effort themselves.

    • Since the age range of Millennials is quite broad, we have used behavioral archetypes to better understand the needs and preferences of our target audience and tailor the platform accordingly. I focused on the Cook Enthousiast and the application will need the following:
  • ustomized recipe recommendations for dietary preferences and cooking style.
  • Reliable nutritional information and clear instructions.
  • Time-saving features for meal planning and grocery shopping.
    • During this time we also generated different names for the application as well with the help of chatGPT. We did this until we landed on: Recipeazy.


    Visualizing

    week 3/4

    We made sure everyone was on the same page by creating mood boards that were linked to the behavioral archetypes. To further refine the archetypes and focus on behavior, we then made adjustments. Additionally, we developed concept boards, storyboards, and a customer journey to analyze the product's flow and features. To prioritize the features, we also utilized a Moscow approach. Once we reached a consensus, we created the logo and based the stylescape on it.





    To design Recipeazy, an app for health-conscious cooks, I created a moodboard with images that reflect the needs and preferences of target group audience. I also made a fresh and fun logo with a green leaf and playful font. Then, I used the Moscow method to prioritize the app's features into four categories: must have, should have, could have, and won't have. This helped me focus on developing essential features first to deliver value to the target users. I focused on creating the system that uses artificial intelligence to generate recipes.



    First Steps

    Based on my research, I have concluded that ChatGPT is the ideal AI technology to generate personalized recipes. To complement this, I used Dall-e to generate images of these personalized recipes. Both of these services are provided by OpenAI through their respective APIs. By leveraging these APIs, I was able to create an application that performs the required tasks of generating personalized recipes and images. The OpenAI offers a range of generation models that are capable of various tasks.

    Developing application

    I utilized React and Next.js to create the recipe generator website, as these technologies allow for seamless page updates and easy conversion to a Progressive Web App. To kickstart the development process, I followed an openai tutorial on creating a superhero name generator for pets. That tutorial was then put into the public ChatGPT as a prompt and at first it generated irrelevant code, but after trail and error with a lot of back and forth, I was able to create the first version of the Recipeazy prototype. By conversing and itterating, I automatically started to understand how the API endpoint worked. Despite undergoing continuous development, the current version still experiences some bugs, primarily due to high traffic on the chatgpt website. These errors will be addressed in the next iteration of the application. Finally, I created a Discord bot in Python that allows users to communicate with a virtual chef. This bot leverages the capabilities of Chat GPT and offers a unique user experience.
