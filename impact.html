Impact

What is the impact of this new technology?

The clear impact of this project is giving more freedom and independence to people that are bound by wheelchair. But what does it mean to have freedom and independence? Feeling free is different for every single person. It is not an achievement you unlock that you don't have to worry about after receiving it. Freedom is rather a state of mind, a state that you need to nurture and truly believe.

Freedom and independence

At a young age, we are taught to be independent of our parents. Learning how to dress ourselves every day and taking steps to become an independent adult. In those early years, you learn to create building blocks for a self-sufficient working adult, an adult that does not need any help to get through life.

Independence is easily defined with clear lines, but following these clear lines is not always that easy. People that are unfortunate enough to be bound by a wheelchair are not able to go through the day-to-day life without any intervention from their friends/family/caretakers.

Being independent will empower the individuals that are bound by wheelchairs, taking control of their lives and being part of society. Inclusivity does not stop at race, sex, or religion, but it should also include people in less fortunate positions.

Freedom is something entirely different. As freedom is a state of mind, one can attain freedom by merely believing that one is free to do whatever they wanted. Needs and desires differ from one another.

After reading “What does it mean to be free?” by Students For Liberty organization I found a perfect description of freedom:

“To be FREE is to be cared for, humanized, trusted, guided, driven by the good, named, self-regulated, charitable, enlightened, reasonable, self-directed, believed, incalculable, indispensable, open, advised, by equals who have neither the right nor wisdom nor virtue to command and control the course of our lives. To be FREE is to be at every moment, in every human exchange, given the opportunity to choose, to be counted on, requested, de-objectified, self-evaluated, individualized, appreciated, considered competent, self-authorized, respected, unencumbered, whole, informed, rewarded. It is, without pretense, and as a matter of fact, to be an essential contributor to our collective well-being, self-disciplined, secure, ennobled, collaborated with and enriched by the experience; then, just as it seems it could not get any better, ever grateful to be alive, to be uplifted, gifted, praised, supported, protected, cherished, defended, empowered, unconstrained, relieved, liberated, helped, accepted, strengthened, accommodated, exalted, dignified, committed to; like nothing else, to be built up, commended, distinguished, blessed, honored. That is freedom; that is justice; that is morality.”
