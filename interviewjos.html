Interview with Jos from the Effenaar

At this point in time we had many questions about these hybrid festivals and what Effenaar expects us to create. What are the requirements? In order for us to get answers on these critical questions, we conducted an interview with Jos from the Effenaar. A creative director at the Effenaar that is in touch with all of the venues, including the Smart Venue and Hit The City.

1. What is the reason you want there to be an online version of the festival?
Because i believe hybrid is the future. Not music business but i am from the business side. I am interested in what is going on what is the future? Additional layer that is gonna be important mayor labels are going to be interesting into gaming.
2. Do you have any in formation about the audience of the online concerts you hosted?
(No information) 15-20 its not the age that defines the audience its the nicse partly designers partly professionals, promoted through dutch design week.
3. Do (online) visitors have to pay for the festival?
Top up? Pay to enter the online festival. No vr/ar but more looking at screens.
4. What kind of style do you want? ( Effenaar style or something unique)
Hit the city style ideally building blocks use for more festivals
5. Can there be an interaction between artists and audience? What will this interaction look like?
Distracted the artist not alot of feedback. The artist takes control what do they want to do? Maybe a chat booth for contact.
6. Should we stay away from live stream kind of interaction?
Don’t know could be interesting to have a connection. Could be a test 3d camera in audience ect.
7. Is there any specific budget we need to keep in mind?
No budget, don’t let yourself get hindered. Just think about the concepts
8. What could we do with pop-up stores at festivals?
Digital merch could be a idea. Its not all stages could implement more.
9. Are there any wishes about vr/ar or not at all?
Elements in it but not totally AR or VR
