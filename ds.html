Disccussion Sparkers

Understading the motivations behind going to a festival that I learned from the "Gezellig"> research.> I created an interactive experience that will fullfill the intrinsic rewards and or interpersonal rewards. Next to that I kept in mind that the experience can not be Virtual Reality and should rather be on a screen.
As social beings one of the many reasons can be that we want to seek for a shared experience and meet like minded people. Spontaneous social interations can be stimulated by creating an experience that allows for this to happen.

When thinking about spontaneous interaction I wanted to come up with a solution that tapped into the following elements:
- The need to voice your opinion
- The feeling of belonging
- Visualizing the results(of a large group) A good way to gather insight is via a survey, however to share the opinion of an entire group I needed to come up with an innovative concept to visualize this.

The survey:
As I have mentioned in , creating a space allows the opportunity for spontaneous interactions and hopefully sparks a conversation, this can be done by asking simple and concise questions. Questions related to the festival or related to the artists. I would not advise picking any controversial topics or sensitive subjects. These questions can be selected by the artist and the Effenaar.

Output of the results:
What are we going to do with the results of the survey?
Just like any other voting system with percentages. Bars that represent with two colors and each color represents one of the answers.

Installation It could be projected on a screen or it can be shown in a physical structure or installation. In order for me to visualize the results of survey in a fun and abstract way, I decided to expriment with different animations in Adobe XD.

First version

For the first version of the Disccussion/Conversation Sparkers I created a visual with the top most listened songs by Drake. The questions on the fake survey could be asking the festival visitors their favorite song by Drake. As soon as the visitors entered a poll at the beginning of the festival, they can see a physical representation about the results. Hopefully this creates an environment that opens up the opportunity for spontaneous interaction.

Second Version

For the second version I applied the same survey questions, but then with the albums of Muse. The vertical triangles represent the amount of times the festival visitors voted on their favorite album.

Third Version

For the last version I kept the same results and artist, but then visualised in circles. The higher the amount of votes for an album, the bigger the album "circle".

Conversation Sparkers Physical Structure

Many big festivals have these giant sculptures or structures that pull the audience and give them an “Otherworldly” experience. A mechanical structure would be fun to create but can be quite a daunting task. A projector would still be too flat. Looking at the time constraint at this point in time [4 weeks] we decided to stick with a simple structure that contains LED strips. Each LED would represent the results of the survey.

First shape:
The structure needs to be quite large in order to draw people in.
Now, how big and how many frames are we talking about? How many LED’s do we need?

Now that we can visualize the idea, how will the survey take place? What kind of questions should the survey have? How should we show the results with LED’s?

How will the LED’s Behave?

As soon as a festival visitor fills in the online survey, that is available by scanning a QR code located in front of the structure. The results are shown in small increments and the colors correspond with the questions. This shows the users their answers in relation to the other visitors that are there. How and where will the festival visitors view the results?
