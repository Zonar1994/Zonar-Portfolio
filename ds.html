As social beings we seek for a shared experience and meeting like minded people. By all menas, this is not the only reason why people go to festivals. Spontaneous social interations can be triggered by creating an experience that anyone at the festival can interact with. Sharing your experience is curcial here. Freedom of speech can also be applied to festivals, having the room and freedom to express yourself, whichever way you want. A survey can easily convey the opinion of large crowds at festivals. But how will they fill in a survey?
Hit the City is known for sparking conversations about societal subjects. Allowing people to fill in a survey and they can see the results, will give spark a conversation. Allowing for spontaneous social interactions. Installation It could be projected on a screen or it can be shown in a physical structure or installation. In order for me to visualize the results of survey in a fun and abstract way, I decided to expriment with different animations in Adobe XD.

First version

For the first version of the Disccussion/Conversation Sparkers I created a visual with the top most listened songs by Drake. The questions on the fake survey could be asking the festival visitors their favorite song by Drake

Second Version

For the second version I applied the same survey questions, but then with the albums of Muse. The vertical triangles represent the amount of times the festival visitors voted on their favorite album.

Third Version

For the last version I kept the same results and artist, but then visualised in circles. The higher the amount of votes for an album, the bigger the album "circle".
