Personal Challenge



Introduction

I am embarking on a personal challenge to address the limitations in existing large language models. My goal over the next 4.5 months is to understand, implement, and validate the "Tree of Thoughts" (ToT) methodology, aiming to enhance these models in strategic reasoning, exploration, and complex decision-making. It's a journey of discovery, and I'm eager to see where this exploration leads.




Goal of the Challenge

The main objective is to deeply understand the ToT methodology and its potential advantages. This involves understanding its principles, crafting a working model, and evaluating its performance. Secondary objectives encompass a comparative analysis with existing techniques and practical applications in problem-solving.

Primary objective:

  • Understand its underlying principles.
  • Implement a working prototype.
  • Evaluate its performance compared to traditional models.

    • Secondary objectives:
  • Comparative analysis with existing methods
  • Apply the model to various problem-solving tasks.

    • Deliverables

    The challenge promises tangible outputs such as detailed documentation on ToT, a working prototype of the model, and established evaluation metrics. While the focus remains on the backend algorithmic improvements, real-world integration and UI design fall outside the current scope.

    In-Scope:

  • Documentation: A comprehensive report detailing the principles of ToT, the implementation process, and results.
  • Prototype:A working model incorporating the ToT methodology.
  • Evaluation MetricsMetrics to evaluate the performance of the model.

    • Out-of-Scopeinclude:
  • Real-world integration.
  • Comprehensive user interface.

    • Requirements

  • Access to literature and research papers on the ToT methodology.

    Go/No Go Challenge

    What problem does it solve?

    The experiments aim to address the limitations in current language models that are not well-equipped for tasks requiring strategic reasoning, exploration, and complex decision-making.

    How does it solve it?

    This system will possibly be able to solve more complex problems, sole due to the re-evaluating nature of the system. This will create a more powerful assistant that is capable of preforming any task.

    Who is it for?

    This research is intended for AI researchers, data scientists, and enterprises that are interested in enhancing the capabilities of language models for complex problem-solving tasks.

    Why is your solution the best?

    The experiments are designed to be both conclusive and comprehensive, targeting key aspects that are essential for advanced problem-solving. This will create a way to validate the ToT methodology. The capability of backtracking and re-evaluating will allow this system to:

    • Correct its errors
    • Reconsideration of earlier decisions
    • Find the most optimal solution

    How is AI/data used?

    AI, in the form of language models, is central to these experiments. The use of data analytics and statistical methods will assist in a thorough evaluation and comparison of the models.

    What data(sets) does it use?

    The experiments will utilize a variety of problem-solving tasks, potentially including mathematical puzzles, logical queries, and more, to rigorously test the models.

    What is the possible positive AND negative impact?

    Positive Impact: Successful experiments could lead to more capable and versatile language models, widening their applicability in various domains.

    Negative Impact: The complexity introduced by the ToT methodology could make these models more difficult to interpret, posing challenges in scenarios where model explainability is crucial.
