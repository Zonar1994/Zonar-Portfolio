Introduction

In the last eight weeks of Creative Technology, I formed a group with Negin, Eugene, Yue, Maarten and Anouk. The six of us worked on the Client Project together. The design process will be the same as the previous project, with the CTDP Tool.
The Effenaar gave us the design challenge that resonated with us: Music Festivals. The Effenaar wants us to merge the gap between an online and live festival. And they wanted us to start experimenting with the Hit The City festival.
Luckily the Effenaar already had live streaming capabilities on their own hosting service, sessions. The Smart Venue, one of the experimental departments from the Effenaar, experiments with new and emerging technologies. The session streaming platform showcases these experiments whenever they come out. This is accompanied by live performances and an interview with the creators.

Flower Brainstorm

The Session platform already has a live streaming function and they are experimenting with virtual rooms in a 3D environment. A hybrid festival, what is that really? Does this have to be virtual reality? Augmented Reality? What type of interactions would there be? How would we merge the online world with the physical world?
Looking at the flower brainstorm on the right, it was clear to use that a hybrid festival can be done in many different ways. There are already Virtual Festivals happening all over the world. One of these "Hybrid" festivals is called Splendour, a festival with an physical festival and a virtual counterpart. People can navigate with their own personalized avatar and enjoy the festivals virtually and physical, while having the same experience. The online counterpart can be exprienced with a Virtual Reality headset and for the ones without these headsets, there is still a live stream of the festival on their website.
Knowing that Virtual Headseets is still not being used enough and you need a capable pc to have a decent experience, it was best not the go for a method that uses these headsets.


The following words were the ones that stood out the most from this Flower Brainstorm: Festival, Entertaining and interactive.

Physical festival vs Online Festival

There are still many aspects that are connected to this hybrid festival. Is it important that the festival visitors, whether online or physical, should have the same experience? It should share the same core values, but not be the exact same thing. In order for us to create an exact replica of a physical festival experience, we would have to use Virtual Reality. And that is simply not feasible, not only do we not have the skill and expertise, virtual reality headsets are currently not readily available or affordable for the average person.

These limtations change the vision and opens doors for new possibilities. Creating a world in VR would be ideal, but a flat screen is a difficult challenge. Understand the current boundaries, we must make a distinction between these two worlds. This will narrow down our scope and gives us room to experiment with different technologies. In my previous projects I added new insights to my vision, but I never subtracted them. I know now when developing a vision, one must add new insights, but also know when to let go of previously gathered insights.

These are the keypoints I concluded should be added to the vision:

  • Physical and online should be different
  • Virtual Reality is not an option
  • Interaction is key
  • Festival feeling is necessary

Chat booth / Meet & Greet box:

A physical booth that the artist can use to interact with their online fans. By using timeslots the artist can video call with their online fans. Multiple fans can join the call at the same time. There is a moderator that mutes/unmutes people to guide the conversation.

Festival Feeling

Before I dove into the interactions that happen at the festival. Wether that is at an online festival or physical festival. I wanted to understand what that feeling of festival brings you. What is that festival feeling?

Online-online - Online-Physical - Online-Artist

There are three types of interactions when it comes to festivals. There are the interactions between the online visitors, the visitors that are the actual festival site and the interactions between the artist and the online/physically present festival visitors.

I decided to work on the interaction between the artist and the festival visitors(online and physically present). Creating an experience that allows visitors to interact with the artist can be one of the intrinsic rewards to have a shared experience with their favorite artist.

Disccussion sparkers

Understading the motivations behind going to a festival that I learned from the "Gezellig"> research.> I created an interactive experience that will fullfill the intrinsic rewards and or interpersonal rewards. Next to that I kept in mind that the experience can not be Virtual Reality and should rather be on a screen.
As social beings one of the many reasons can be that we want to seek for a shared experience and meet like minded people. Spontaneous social interations can be stimulated by creating an experience that allows for this to happen.

Body Projection Mapping

That moment when you and your friends appear on the big screens at the festival, this can be a way to create an opportunity to allow interaction to happen between the visitors and the interaction between the visitors and preformers. This is done spontaneously and people have no idea of knowing when they would appear on those screens. What if a festival or concert allowed the audience to project their face on the big screen by using their phone camera.
Of course that would be a terrible idea, people would misuse and therefore would not be ideal. But what if you could allow the audience to project stickers or animations on the preformers?

Second pitch for the Effenaar

The Effenaar loved the idea of the Disccussion Sparkers and the Body Projection Mapping. When done right, creating interaction between the visitors and artists can fullfill the intrinsic and personal rewards that festival visitors are looking for. We decided to continue with the two ideas. We had many ideas for Effenaar design challenge, but we were glad that we prioritized our time to just two ideas.
