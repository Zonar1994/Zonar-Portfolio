Introduction

In the last eight weeks of Creative Technology, I formed a group with Negin, Eugene, Yue, Maarten and Anouk. The six of us worked on the Client Project together. The design process will be the same as the previous project, with the CTDP Tool.
The Effenaar gave us the design challenge that resonated with us: Music Festivals. The Effenaar wants us to merge the gap between an online and live festival. And they wanted us to start experimenting with the Hit The City festival.
Luckily the Effenaar already had live streaming capabilities on their own hosting service, sessions. The Smart Venue, one of the experimental departments from the Effenaar, experiments with new and emerging technologies. The session streaming platform showcases these experiments whenever they come out. This is accompanied by live performances and an interview with the creators.

The Effenaar

The Effenaar is a multifunctional pop stage with two halls, a restaurant-cafe and its own backyard. However, they also have programs throughout Eindhoven that enhance the atmosphere of a concert, a skate park or the "Klokgebouw". The Effenaar also orginaizes festivals with international allure such as Eindhoven Metal Meeting, Come As You Are, DDW Music Fuzz Club and Hit The City. The festival that we need to start with, but the solution should be applicable to a wider range of festivals, not just Hit The City

What is Hit the City

Hit the city is a venue that allows upcoming artist to perform in the city of Eindhoven, at the Effenaar and various cafe's throughout the city. The genre's range from Indie to hip-hop to garage and singer-songwriters.

The main purpose for venues are to promote new artists and engage conversations about sensitive, controversial and political subject. Because they are open to- and promote a safe environment for these conversations, the topics at hand have a wide range. Some examples of this would be gender diversity or something like an economic crisis. The Effenaar shared to following video with us, to shows us the vibe of the Hit the City festivals/venues.

What is the Session Platform?

The Sessions is a live-platform created to showcase and support bands and musicians online. It started as The Isolation Sessions in march 2020 at the beginning of the lockdown. The Sessions are intended to allow musicians and audiences to enjoy live music in this worldwide crisis on a daily basis. The Sessions is a collaboration between Dynamo, Effenaar, Copyright Delta and of course, all the amazing musicians.

Effenaar promoter Robert Schaeffer said the following about Sessions: "The Effenaar is the amplifier of the pop culture of Eindhoven and this collaboration fits in perfectly. We strive to bring the passion of the musicians to the audience homes. During the sessions the guidelines of the RIVM are of course meticulously observed”.

Flower Brainstorm

Our group decided to do a flower brainstorm to get started on the project. Something we kept in mind douring this was that The Session platform already has a live streaming function and they are experimenting with virtual rooms in a 3D environment. There are a few elements about this brainstorming-session I’d like to highlight because its shows how we originally started forming our vision.

When thinking about a hybrid festival we started by thinking about what this meant to ourselves. Douring our brainstorming, we found ourselves asking the following questions:

- Does this have to be virtual reality?
- Does this have to be Augmented Reality?
- What type of interactions would there be?
- How would we merge the online world with the physical world?

Looking at the flower brainstorm, it was clear to us that a hybrid festival can be done in many different ways. There are already Virtual Festivals happening all over the world. One of these "Hybrid" festivals is called Splendour, a festival with an physical festival and a virtual counterpart. People can navigate with their own personalized avatar and enjoy the festivals virtually and physical, while having the same experience. The online counterpart can be experienced with a Virtual Reality headset and for the ones without these headsets, there is still a live stream of the festival on their website. We also realized that Virtual Headsets are still not being widely used and you need a capable pc to have a decent experience. That combined with the fact this is something that other venues are doing with a greater level of expertise than we have, we decided it was best not the go for a method that uses Virtual headsets.

The following words were the ones that stood out the most from this Flower Brainstorm: Festival, Entertaining and interactive.

Physical festival vs Online Festival

There are still many aspects that are connected to this hybrid festival. At first we asked ourselves: ‘Is it important that the festival visitors, whether online or physical, should have the same experience?’. We concluded that though it should share the same core values, it does not have to be the exact same thing. In order for us to create an exact replica of a physical festival experience, we would likely have to use Virtual Reality, which we previously ruled out for various reasons.

By limiting ourselves, we change the vision and opened doors to new possibilities. To work within our current boundaries, we must make a distinction between the online and physical experience and the tools we can use to enhance and merge them separately. This will narrow down our scope and gives us room to experiment with different technologies. In my previous projects I added new insights to my vision, but I never subtracted them. I know now when developing a vision, one must add new insights, but also know when to let go of ideas to set yourself on a more accurate course towards developing a project.

Shaping my vision

As we were progressing, I decided it was time to compare what I learned so far in the previous steps:

  • looking into everything we knew about Effenaar, Hit the City and The Session
  • the interview with the Effenaar
  • the brainstorming session
  • the distinction between the physical and online world


Based on everything we learned I wrote down a couple key points about hour vision to keep in mind while shaping our project:
  • physical and online should be tackled differently
  • virtual Reality is not an option
  • interaction is key
  • a festival feeling is necessary, within both worlds
  • it needs to be applicable for a wide range of festivals

First Pitch

Due to the amount of freedom and the short amount of time we had for the first pitch, we decided to come up with three quick ideas for our first pitch to the rest of the class. These first ideas, are very simple and are already being used by many different live streaming platforms and/or live concerts. At this point we had not settled on a particular idea, and were still exploring what was out there. What we did know is that we should focus on a specific problem to tackle:

Problem definition: How might we make an online festival as interactive and attractive as its physical counterpart.

Tagline: The future is Hybrid

Chat booth / Meet & Greet box:

A physical booth that the artist can use to interact with their online fans. By using timeslots the artist can video call with their online fans. Multiple fans can join the call at the same time. There is a moderator that mutes/unmutes people to guide the conversation.

Festival Feeling

Before I dove into the interactions that happen at the festival, whether that is at an online- or a physical festival, I wanted to understand what that feeling of festival brings you. What is that festival feeling? You can read all about my research and conclusions via the link in the title.

Online-online - Online-Physical - Online-Artist

There are three types of interactions when it comes to festivals. There are the interactions between the online visitors, the visitors that are the actual festival site and the interactions between the artist and the online/physically present festival visitors.

I decided to work on the interaction between the artist and the festival visitors(online and physically present). Creating an experience that allows visitors to interact with the artist can be one of the intrinsic rewards to have a shared experience with their favorite artist.

Disccussion sparkers

Understading the motivations behind going to a festival that I learned from the "Gezellig"> research.> I created an interactive experience that will fullfill the intrinsic rewards and or interpersonal rewards. Next to that I kept in mind that the experience can not be Virtual Reality and should rather be on a screen.
As social beings one of the many reasons can be that we want to seek for a shared experience and meet like minded people. Spontaneous social interations can be stimulated by creating an experience that allows for this to happen.

Body Projection Mapping

That moment when you and your friends appear on the big screens at the festival, this can be a way to create an opportunity to allow interaction to happen between the visitors and the interaction between the visitors and preformers. This is done spontaneously and people have no idea of knowing when they would appear on those screens. What if a festival or concert allowed the audience to project their face on the big screen by using their phone camera.
Of course that would be a terrible idea, people would misuse and therefore would not be ideal. But what if you could allow the audience to project stickers or animations on the preformers?
