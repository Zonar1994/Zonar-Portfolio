Introduction

In the last eight weeks of Creative Technology, I formed a group with Negin, Eugene, Yue, Maarten and Anouk. The six of us worked on the Client Project together. The design process will be the same as the previous project, with the CTDP Tool.
The Effenaar gave us the design challenge that resonated with us: Music Festivals. A creative director from the Effenaar presented a rather interesting design challenge to us that resonated with us.
The Corona pandemic catapulted the growing demand of live streams and put a lot of venues at risk. Luckily the Effenaar already had live streaming capabilities on their own hosting service, sessions. The Smart Venue, one of the experimental departments from the Effenaar, experiments with new and emerging technologies. The session streaming platform showcases these experiments whenever they come out. This is accompanied by live performances and an interview with the creators.

Effenaar

De Effenaar is a multifunctional pop stage with two halls, a restaurant-café and its own backyard. However, we also program in places in Eindhoven that enhance the atmosphere of a concert; a skate park, a church, the Klokgebouw or a green part of the city such as Groendomein het Wasven. Besides the concert and dance programming, we organize festivals with international allure such as Eindhoven Metal Meeting, Come As You Are, DDW Music and Fuzz Club.

Hit the City

A venue that allows upcoming new artist to preform in the city of Eindhoven. Preforming at the Effenaar and various cafe's throughout Eindhoven. The genre's range form Indie, hip-hop, garage and singer-songwriters.
The main purpose for venues are to promote new artists and engage converstations about sensitive, controversial and political subject. From gender neutrality to economical crisis, all of these subjects are brought up at these venues. The following video shows the vibe of the Hit the City festivals/venues.

Sessions

The Sessions is a live-platform created to showcase and support bands and musicians online. It started as The Isolation Sessions in march 2020 at the beginning of the corona-lockdown. The sessions are intended to allow musicians and audiences to enjoy live music in this worldwide crisis on a daily basis. Effenaar promoter Robert Schaeffer: "The Effenaar is the amplifier of the pop culture of Eindhoven and this collaboration fits in perfectly. We strive to bring the passion of the musicians to the audience homes. During the sessions the guidelines of the RIVM are of course meticulously observed.” The Sessions is a collaboration between Dynamo, Effenaar, Copyright Delta and of course: all the amazing musicians.

Design Challenge

The concept for a virtual festival site containing different functionalities/experiences for visitors visiting Hit the City

These functionalities should be applicable to other festivals and venues that Effenaar supports.

Flower Brainstorm

The Session platform already has a live streaming function and they are experimenting with virtual rooms in a 3D environment. This gives us a lot of room for creativity and is why I decided to brainstorm new ideas for this project. This was done by using the Flower Brainstorm method.

Physical festival vs Online Festival

There are still many aspects that are connected to this hybrid festival. Is it important that the festival visitors, whether online or physical, should have the same experience? It should share the same core values, but not be the exact same thing. In order for us to create an exact replica of a physical festival experience, we would have to use Virtual Reality. And that is simply not feasible, not only do we not have the skill and expertise, virtual reality headsets are currently not readily available or affordable for the average person. Understand the current boundaries, we must make a distinction between these two worlds. This increases the feasibility and gives us room to experiment with different technologies.

Now that we have established the following ground rules:

  • Physical and online should be different
  • Virtual Reality is not an option
  • Interaction is key
  • Festival feeling is necessary

First Pitch

Due to the amount of freedom that we had to create new experiences for hybrid festivals, we decided to come up with three quick ideas for our first pitch to the rest of the class. These first ideas are very simple and are already being used by many different live streaming platforms or live concerts. At this point we could not phantom any other idea for this vastly broad topic.

Problem definition: How might we make an online festival as interactive and attractive as its physical counterpart.

Tagline: The future is Hybrid

Festival map:

This map allows the audience at home to get an overview of the live performances that are at a festival. The user is able to select the desired performance and experience it at home. This map can be exchanged for any map. All stages/booths can be assigned on this map. This map contains a chat where people can converse with each other. An avatar will be displayed on the map based on where you are virtually visiting the festival. Physical visitors can sign into the app and use their GPS to show their avatar on the map.

Live festival with chat function:

An online platform that hosts the live performances of the festival. This platform contains a chat box that allows the audience at home to interact with each other. Each stage can have one of these chat boxes. Every artist can customize their chat (emoji only, customize emojis, etc.)

Chat booth / Meet & Greet box:

A physical booth that the artist can use to interact with their online fans. By using timeslots the artist can video call with their online fans. Multiple fans can join the call at the same time. There is a moderator that mutes/unmutes people to guide the conversation.

Splitting up the tasks

This semester there were two other groups that worked on the same Effenaar project. After the second interview with the Effenaar, which was also done together with the other two groups, we decided unanimously that we should split the workload between the three Effenaar groups. Interaction, Navigation and Personalization were the three aspects that were divided between the groups. Our group decided to work on the interaction for a hybrid music festival.
