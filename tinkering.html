Exploring the Neural Headset

The Emotiv Epoc x is a neural headset that records brain activity by using the EEG (Electroencephalography). The software is available on the Emotiv website and allows us to connect the headset to a PC. One of the programs that communicate with the headsets is EmotivBCI (Brain Computer Interface).

Fitting the headset

We started off by wetting the pads that were included in the kit. These pads are then placed in the 16 Sensor Nodes and two rubber nobs for Reference Points. The headset can be fitted on the head in two orientations. The headband is in the back of the head or on top. Once the correct orientation is set, we can move on to the Contact Quality. The Contact Quality instructions show us if the Nodes on the headset have optimal contact quality. This step is crucial for getting the best results. When all the Node Sensors make optimal contact with the skin, we can now move on to the next step. Once we have optimal contact with the skin, we can now move on to training the headset.

EmotivBCI

The EMOTIV BCI is used to train Mental Commands, view real-time Performance Metrics, Facial Expression, and Motion Sensor data streams from the headsets.The EmotivBCI program gives instructions on the placement of the headset. Once the headset is in the correct position, the training program starts. The training program allows users to register predetermined commands to the brain activity. It is not capable of “reading our minds”, but it is essentially a training program that allows users to assign brain activity to certain commands or actions

After some training, we discovered that it is quite draining to keep sending these commands. We discovered we can also use facial expressions. This gave us the idea to use the expressions for navigating the application and use the mental commands to control specific smart home devices. Besides that, the stream of the facial expressions are a lot faster. This makes it easier to use it for navigation.
