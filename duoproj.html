Neural Home

Duo Project

Introduction

After the fun and explorative train vibrations project, I moved on to the DUO Project. In this project, I partnered up with Maarten, a software student that I connected with on the first day of this specialization. We decided early that we wanted to continue Maarten’s project. He developed an application that utilized Eye Tracking software to control smart devices. Maarten was developing this for a friend of his that is Quadriplegic and therefore is bound to a wheelchair. With this unfortunate situation, Maarten was able to effectively give his friend more freedom and independence. This blew me away at the expo for the personal projects. Together with my love for home automation and helping people, I offered to continue the development of Maarten’s project.

How will I be tackeling this?

Creative Technology Design Process tool

    This semester we used the Creative Technology Design Process tool to combine the engineering design process and the design thinking process. The CTDP tool consists of five phases, Trigger, Ideation, Specification & Design, Realization, and Evaluation.

Trigger

Interest

Home automation and helping people

    As I’ve mentioned in my introduction, Maarten and I had a mutual interest in home automation and helping people. We continued with his concept and vision.

The "perfect world

    A world where people and technology have a symbiotic relationship. Working alongside each other and providing an ethical and impactful experience. Preventing technology from becoming exploitive and damaging to the quality of life. The newest emerging technologies used to excite me. The biggest investments are put into digitizing human interaction in the last couple of years. That is the exact opposite of my vision. Technology should not replace our most basic social interaction. Touch, presence, and energy. Next to that, I want to create a world that develops technology in an ethical and impactful way and ensures that everyone can benefit from that new technology. That is where the creativity comes in, not only focusing on making something fun and useful but also making sure that anyone can use it!

How Might We?

Let's rephase those design challenges

    In my vision I mentioned that new technology should enhance our lives in an ethical and impactful way. To ensure that these factors are properly accounted for, I used a research method. “How Might We” is rephrasing a known challenge and dividing the important aspects.

Interview

Insights on our target group audience

    In order to get better insisghts on how Quadriplegic users interact with technology in their day to day lives, Maarten and I set up an Interview with someone with that condition. This was a friend of Maarten and he was more than welcome to answer all of our questions.

Persona

Target group

    The interview gave us insights on how a quadriplegic user interacts with technology in their daily lives. In some cases quadriplegic patients can experience speech impediment due to the paralyzation. Therefore being unable to use speech recognition software to control their devices.

Impact

What is the impact of this new technology?

    The clear impact of this project is giving more freedom and independence to people that are bound by wheelchair. But what does it mean to have freedom and independence? Feeling free is different for every single person. It is not an achievement you unlock that you don't have to worry about after receiving it. Freedom is rather a state of mind, a state that you need to nurture and truly believe.

Neural Headset Research

What are we working with here?

    Now that we know that not all cases of quadriplegia cases are the same. In many cases there is some mobility in the arms and legs but not enough for precise movement and reliable. In the case of the project, we want to allow a specific kind of target group to contorl their smart home devices. But how?

    That is where the Neural Headset came in. Before I dove into the a device of such caliber, I decided to research neural headset.

Tinkering

Exploring the Neural Headset

After Understanding how we can use the Emotiv Epoc X Headset, I started tinkering with the headset and to see if this headset will be reliable and ultimately applicable for our users. The Emotiv Epoc requires a primer fluid for the electrodes and that would not be the most ideal situation. The caretaker would have to reply this primer fluid every hour. This will result in less independence and freedom for the user. Luckily Nextmind offers a Neural Headset without the need of primer fluid and it is easier to put on. Making the set up process faster and easier for the user.

Requirements

MoSCoW Method

    A prioritization technique used to reach a common understanding with stakeholders on the importance they place on the delivery of each requirement.

    The final prototype should at least be able to toggle a smart home device on and off.

Storyboard

How will the interaction with the Neural headset be?

    In order for the users give full control to smart home devices, I decided to impliment a dedicated screen with an user interface for the smart home devices. In this storyboard you can see how the interaction between the user and the application will take place. Using facial expressions to navigate the application and mental Commands to toggle lights. Due to the frequency of the data stream coming from the facial expression, it was decided that the users shoudl navigate the application using the Facial Expressions.

System flow

How will the user interact with the application by using the user interace?

    For our target group audience it will always be necessary to apply the Emotiv Headset at the start of the day. The dedicated screen that will run the Neural Home application and Emotiv BCI, this is mounted on the wall

User Interface

Allowing the user control smart devices with facial expressions and mental commands

    Giving quadriplegic users control over their smart home devices by using a neural headset, can be quite challenging. In order for the user to have an overview and know what their are doing, it was improtant to have an interface. My experience from my previous home automation project taught me to keep it simple and easy to use.
